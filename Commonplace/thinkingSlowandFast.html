Thinking Slow and Fast, Daniel Kahneman

"Thinking, Fast and Slow" is a non-fiction book written by Daniel Kahneman, a psychologist and economist, published in 2011. The book is an exploration of the psychology of decision making and the different ways in which our minds process information. The book is based on the research of the author and his colleague Amos Tversky, who have been pioneers in the field of behavioral psychology and cognitive biases. The book is divided into two parts: the first one focuses on how our mind works in two different systems, System 1 and System 2. System 1 is fast, intuitive, and automatic, while System 2 is slow, deliberate, and effortful. The second part of the book focuses on the cognitive biases and heuristics that affect our decision making. The main themes of the book include the idea that our minds have two distinct systems for processing information, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. The book also explains how cognitive biases and heuristics, such as availability heuristic, framing effect, halo effect, sunk cost fallacy, hindsight bias, base-rate fallacy, and affect heuristic, can affect our decision making. The author also emphasizes the importance of being aware of these biases and heuristics and the need to use System 2 thinking in order to make rational decisions. Overall, "Thinking, Fast and Slow" is a comprehensive guide to the psychology of decision making, it provides valuable insights and strategies for anyone looking to improve their decision-making skills and be more aware of the cognitive biases that can affect their judgment.
