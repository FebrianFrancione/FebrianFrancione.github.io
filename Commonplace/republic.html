The Republic, Plato

In the case of "The Republic" by Plato, a commonplace book might include key quotes from the text, summaries of important arguments or ideas, and personal reflections or notes on the book's themes and meaning. Some possible entries for a "The Republic" commonplace book might include: A quote from Socrates on the nature of justice: "Until philosophers rule as kings or those who are now called kings and leading men genuinely and adequately philosophize, that is, until political power and philosophy entirely coincide, while the many natures who at present pursue either one exclusively are forcibly prevented from doing so, cities will have no rest from evils, ... nor, I think, will the human race." A summary of Plato's theory of the Forms, and how it relates to the idea of justice. Personal reflection on the book's concept of the "noble lie" and its implications for politics and society. Notes on the character of Thrasymachus and how he represents the traditional, non-philosophic view of justice. Quotes on the concept of the philosopher-king and how it relates to the idea of a just society. Summary of the allegory of the cave and its significance in the book. Personal reflection on the concept of the good and how it relates to the idea of justice. Quotes on the concept of education and its role in achieving a just society. Summary of the different types of government discussed in the book and their relationship to justice. Personal reflection on Plato's idea of the soul and how it relates to the concept of justice.
